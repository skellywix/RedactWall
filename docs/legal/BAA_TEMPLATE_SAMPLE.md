# Business Associate Agreement (BAA) — SAMPLE

> **SAMPLE — NON-BINDING.** Provided for procurement convenience only. This is
> **not legal advice** and creates no obligation. Execute only after review by
> your legal counsel. A BAA is required only where the Processor handles PHI on
> behalf of a HIPAA covered entity; many credit-union deployments do not.

## 1. Parties
- **Covered Entity:** [entity], to the extent it is a HIPAA covered entity.
- **Business Associate:** [RedactWall entity].

## 2. Permitted uses and disclosures
- The Business Associate may use or disclose Protected Health Information (PHI)
  only to provide the RedactWall service and as Required by Law. RedactWall's
  design **minimizes** PHI exposure: detection is on-device and exports carry
  masked findings, hashes, and metadata rather than PHI.

## 3. Safeguards (HIPAA Security Rule, 45 CFR 164)
- The Business Associate implements administrative, physical, and technical
  safeguards: AES-256-GCM encryption at rest for retained data, MFA and
  step-up authentication for administrative access, and a tamper-evident,
  hash-chained audit trail. It uses the minimum necessary PHI.

## 4. Subcontractors
- The Business Associate ensures any subcontractor that creates, receives,
  maintains, or transmits PHI agrees to restrictions at least as strict as
  those in this BAA. The local-first deployment uses no default subcontractors.

## 5. Breach notification
- The Business Associate reports any use/disclosure not permitted by this BAA,
  any Security Incident, and any Breach of Unsecured PHI without unreasonable
  delay and no later than **[30] days** (sooner where the Covered Entity's
  regulatory clock, e.g., NCUA 72-hour reporting, requires).

## 6. Access, amendment, accounting
- The Business Associate makes PHI available to satisfy the Covered Entity's
  obligations under 45 CFR 164.524, 164.526, and 164.528.

## 7. Termination and return/destruction
- On termination, the Business Associate returns or destroys all PHI where
  feasible; where infeasible, protections are extended and further use limited.

## 8. Governing law
- Governing law: [state]. This BAA is interpreted to permit compliance with
  HIPAA and applicable NCUA/GLBA requirements.
